# Essay & Model Workshop — Best Practices

This file accumulates lessons learned from working in the system. Append to it as patterns emerge. Don't rewrite history; add dated notes.

## What makes a good Idea row

- **Specific working title.** "Why Atlassian's product surface is a tell" beats "Atlassian essay." The future-Ray reading the inbox should be able to remember what he meant without opening the page.
- **A capturable thesis in one or two sentences in the page body.** Not a draft. Just enough that the argument can be reconstructed without the original conversation. Test: if you delete the Source Link, can the row still be understood?
- **A controlling metaphor if one exists.** Ray's essays live or die by the metaphor. If the conversation produced one (castle, lab coat, bridge, water-vs-bucket), capture it. If not, leave the field blank — a weak metaphor is worse than no metaphor.
- **Source Link.** Always. The original conversation is the highest-fidelity source and Ray will want to find it again.

## What makes a good Seed model

- **The name carries weight.** "Outside-advisor ceiling" is a name; "the thing about advisors" is not. If the name isn't there yet, mark the row with a descriptive placeholder in quotes — "the trap where the founder runs out of permission to renarrate" — easier to find later than a vague label.
- **Definition in one sentence.** If you can't compress it, it's not ready to be a model — it's still an observation. That's fine; capture as Seed status and the definition field can stay empty until workshopping clarifies it.
- **The Move and Counter-Pattern are what make a model usable.** A model without these is a label. With them, it's a tool. Get at least one of the two filled in at capture time when possible.
- **Origin matters.** Where the model came from is part of its credential. "Surfaced in Xano analysis, Dec 2025" is more useful than no origin. The origin field tells future-Ray whether the model is field-tested or speculative.

## When essay and model co-occur

Most of Ray's best material spawns both: the conversation that produces an essay also names a model the essay uses. Capture both. Link them via the Related Essays / Related Models relation. The pair is more valuable than either alone.

When in doubt about which is which: if Ray would write about it, it's an essay. If Ray would use it to think with, it's a model.

## Extraction passes from past chats

- **One chat at a time.** Don't try to mine three chats at once — the proposed list gets too long and Ray vetoes everything to clear the noise.
- **Cap proposals at ~5 per chat.** If a chat produced more than 5 candidates, the chat probably contains a meta-pattern worth one entry, not five entries.
- **Surface the strongest first.** Lead with the one or two candidates that have a clear name or clear thesis. The weaker candidates get judged against the strongest, and Ray can drop the bottom of the list without thinking hard.
- **Don't propose models for one-line observations.** A model needs a move or a counter-pattern at minimum. A passing observation in a chat is not a model; it's an observation. It can be an essay idea, though.

## What "Mentor Ready" actually means

A model is mentor-ready when:
1. It has a one-sentence definition that doesn't require context to understand
2. It has a clear move or counter-pattern (ideally both)
3. It has at least two concrete examples someone could recognize
4. The name is the name Ray would use in a session

It is NOT mentor-ready just because Status reached Productionized. Productionized + Mentor Ready are two different signals. Productionized means the model is stable in Ray's thinking. Mentor Ready means it's ready to be used in front of someone else.

## Status transitions — when to push back

Ray sometimes wants to mark things further along than they are. That's fine for his own use, but a few transitions deserve a question:

- **Idea → Draft without an Outline.** Sometimes legitimate (small essays), sometimes a sign the argument hasn't been structured yet. Ask: "want to skip Outline, or do you want to outline first?"
- **Seed → Productionized in one move.** Usually means the model was already mature when captured and the workshop status was wrong. That's fine — confirm and update.
- **Marking Mentor Ready without a Definition filled in.** Always ask. The mentor will surface the model with whatever's in the fields. Missing Definition = missing Definition in the mentor.

## Things to avoid

- **Don't auto-tag with everything that might apply.** Two or three tags is plenty. More than four dilutes the tag system.
- **Don't paste the entire chat into the page body.** A 2-5 sentence summary plus a Source Link is more useful than a transcript dump. The Source Link is for going back to the original; the body is for orientation.
- **Don't capture Ray's own one-liners as essays.** Sometimes a line like "engineers are stationed at fortifications the era no longer needs" is the seed of an essay already in the pipeline — search before creating duplicates.
